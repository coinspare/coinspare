A personal loan is an unsecured loan that helps you meet your current financial needs. You don't usually need to pledge any collateral or security while availing a personal loan. Your lender provides you with the flexibility to use the funds as per your convenience.

  • Online Application: Apply from the comfort of your home

What is a Home Loan?

A home loan is a secured loan that you can avail to purchase or construct a residential property. The property itself serves as collateral for the loan. Home loans typically have longer repayment tenures and lower interest rates compared to personal loans.

Benefits of Home Loans from Finvare

Can I prepay my loan? Are there any charges?

Yes, you can prepay your loan at any time. The prepayment charges vary based on the type of loan and lender policies:

Personal Loans

  • Prepayment charges: Typically 2-5% of the outstanding loan amount
  • When charges apply: Usually within the first 6-12 months of the loan tenure
  • After lock-in period: Many lenders allow prepayment without charges after a certain period
  • Partial prepayment: Allowed with minimum amount restrictions

Home Loans

  • Prepayment charges: Generally lower or no charges for floating rate loans
  • Fixed rate loans: May have prepayment charges of 2-3%
  • Regulatory guidelines: As per RBI guidelines, no prepayment charges on floating rate home loans
  • Partial prepayment: Allowed without restrictions

Business Loans

  • Prepayment charges: Typically 3-5% of the prepaid amount
  • Lock-in period: Usually 6-12 months
  • After lock-in: Charges may reduce or be waived
  • Terms: Vary based on business loan agreement

Before making a prepayment, we recommend checking your loan agreement or contacting our customer service to understand the exact charges applicable to your loan. Prepaying can help you save on interest costs in the long run.

What is the minimum credit score required for a loan?

While a credit score of 750 or above is considered ideal for loan approval, we at Finvare consider applications with credit scores as low as 650. Here's how credit scores impact your loan application:

Credit Score Categories

  • 750 and above: Excellent - High chances of approval with best interest rates
  • 700-749: Good - Good chances of approval with competitive rates
  • 650-699: Fair - May get approval with slightly higher rates
  • 600-649: Poor - Limited options with higher rates and stricter terms
  • Below 600: Very Poor - Difficult to get approval, may need collateral

How to Improve Your Credit Score

  • Pay all your bills and EMIs on time
  • Keep your credit utilization below 30%
  • Maintain a healthy mix of secured and unsecured loans
  • Avoid multiple loan applications in a short period
  • Regularly check your credit report for errors

Options for Low Credit Scores

  • Apply with a co-applicant who has a good credit score
  • Opt for secured loans against collateral
  • Start with a smaller loan amount and build credit history
  • Consider credit counseling to improve your score

At Finvare, we understand that credit scores don't tell the whole story. We consider your overall financial profile, income stability, and repayment capacity while evaluating your loan application.

5 Ways to Improve Your Credit Score

A good credit score is essential for getting loans at favorable interest rates. Here are five effective strategies to improve your credit score:

1. Pay Your Bills on Time

Payment history is the most significant factor in your credit score, accounting for 35% of the total. Set up automatic payments or reminders to ensure you never miss a due date. Even a single late payment can significantly impact your score.

2. Reduce Credit Utilization

Try to keep your credit utilization ratio below 30%. This means if you have a credit limit of ₹1,00,000, try not to use more than ₹30,000 at any time. High credit utilization indicates dependency on credit and can lower your score.

3. Maintain a Mix of Credit Types

Having a healthy mix of secured (home loan, car loan) and unsecured (credit card, personal loan) credit accounts can positively impact your score. It shows lenders that you can manage different types of credit responsibly.

Home Loan vs. Personal Loan: Which is Better?

When you need funds for a home purchase, should you opt for a home loan or use a personal loan? We break down the differences to help you decide:

Interest Rates

Home loans typically have lower interest rates (8-10%) compared to personal loans (10-20%). This is because home loans are secured against the property, reducing the lender's risk.

Loan Amount

Home loans allow you to borrow larger amounts, often up to 80-90% of the property value. Personal loans usually have lower limits, typically up to ₹20-25 lakhs based on your income.

Tenure

Home loans come with longer repayment tenures of up to 30 years, resulting in lower EMIs. Personal loans have shorter tenures, usually 1-5 years, leading to higher EMIs.

Understanding EMI and How to Calculate It

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is a crucial aspect of any loan. Learn how EMIs are calculated and factors that affect your monthly payments:

What is EMI?

EMI is the fixed amount you pay each month towards repaying your loan. It includes both the principal amount and the interest. The EMI remains constant throughout the loan tenure, though the proportion of principal and interest changes over time.

EMI Calculation Formula

The mathematical formula for calculating EMI is: EMI = [P x R x (1+R)^N]/[(1+R)^N-1], where P is the principal amount, R is the monthly interest rate, and N is the number of monthly installments.

Factors Affecting EMI

Your EMI is affected by the loan amount, interest rate, and tenure. Higher loan amounts or interest rates increase your EMI, while longer tenures reduce it. However, longer tenures also mean paying more interest over time.
